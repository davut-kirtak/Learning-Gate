Introduction to Science

Course Overview

The Introduction to Science course is designed to provide students with a broad understanding of the scientific method, principles, and processes. This course serves as an introduction to various scientific disciplines, including biology, chemistry, physics, and environmental science. Students will explore fundamental scientific concepts, conduct hands-on experiments, and develop critical thinking and analytical skills necessary for scientific inquiry.

Course Duration

14 weeks

Course Objectives

  1. To introduce students to the scientific method and its application in different scientific disciplines.
  2. To develop a basic understanding of key scientific concepts and principles.
  3. To foster critical thinking and analytical skills required for scientific investigation and problem-solving.
  4. To familiarize students with laboratory techniques and procedures.
  5. To promote an appreciation for the role of science in addressing real-world challenges and advancements.

Course Outline

  1. Introduction to Science:

    • Scientific method and its components
    • Principles of scientific inquiry
    • Ethics and safety in scientific research

  2. Biology:

    • Cell structure and function
    • Genetics and heredity
    • Evolution and natural selection
    • Ecology and environmental science

  3. Chemistry:

    • Atoms, elements, and compounds
    • Chemical reactions and stoichiometry
    • Acids, bases, and pH
    • States of matter and solutions

  4. Physics:

    • Motion and forces
    • Energy and work
    • Waves and sound
    • Electricity and magnetism

  5. Environmental Science:

    • Ecosystems and biodiversity
    • Climate change and sustainability
    • Renewable and non-renewable resources
    • Environmental impact and conservation
