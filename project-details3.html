World History: A Journey Through Time

Course Overview

The World History course is a captivating journey through time, exploring the major events, civilizations, and cultural developments that have shaped our world. This course provides students with a comprehensive understanding of key historical periods, significant figures, and impactful global phenomena. Through interactive lectures, discussions, and immersive learning activities, students will gain insights into the past and its influence on the present.

Course Duration

16 weeks

Course Objectives

  1. To provide students with a broad understanding of major historical events, eras, and civilizations.
  2. To develop critical thinking and analytical skills through the examination and interpretation of historical sources.
  3. To foster an appreciation for the cultural, social, and political factors that have shaped human history.
  4. To encourage students to recognize the connections between historical events and contemporary issues.
  5. To cultivate effective research, writing, and presentation skills within the context of historical studies.

Course Outline

  1. Ancient Civilizations:

    • Mesopotamia, Egypt, and the Indus Valley
    • Ancient Greece and Rome
    • The Maurya and Gupta Empires
    • The Han Dynasty and Ancient China

  2. Medieval Period:

    • The Byzantine Empire
    • The Islamic Golden Age
    • Feudalism and the Middle Ages in Europe
    • The Crusades

  3. Early Modern Era:

    • The Renaissance and Reformation
    • Exploration and Colonization
    • The Age of Enlightenment
    • The Scientific Revolution

  4. Modern World:

    • The Industrial Revolution
    • The French Revolution and Napoleonic Era
    • Imperialism and Colonialism
    • World Wars and Global Conflicts

  5. Contemporary Issues:

    • The Cold War
    • Decolonization and the Post-Colonial Era
    • The Rise of Globalization
    • Current Challenges and Future Perspectives
